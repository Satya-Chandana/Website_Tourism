About Telangana





Telangana, India's 29th and youngest state, was officially recognized on June 2, 2014, and is home to a plethora of tourist attractions. Telangana is renowned for its hospitality and pluralistic, multicultural culture. Hyderabad, the state's capital, is India's fifth largest city, home to some of the country's best educational institutions, public sector and defence firms, as well as a flourishing global services sector and film industry, and is renowned for its world-famous Hyderabadi Biryani.


Telangana, which makes up a large part of the Deccan plateau, has a pleasant climate and ample natural and water resources. The state is known as India's seed city and is located at the confluence of the Krishna and Godavari rivers in South India. One of the wealthy economies in India, Telangana is here to take its rightful place in the country, as a highly developed state and society with a proud history and grand heritage.

📌

The rulers' patronage and interest for arts and culture transformed Telangana into a unique multi-cultural region where two different cultures coexist together, thus making Telangana the representative of the Deccan Plateau and its heritage with Warangal and Hyderabad being its epicenter.

Telangana apart from the historical perspective of various dynastic and regional and cultural influences offers its visitors a plethora of places of pristine natural beauty. With serene lakes, verdant woods, rocky regions, abundant wildlife, flora and fauna, elaborately carved temples, magnificent palaces and forts reflecting architectural blends of Hindu, Pathan and Persian styles. The artistic temper of the local people finds expression in the varied art forms of the different tribes and regions of Telangana in paintings, handicrafts in wood and metal and textile of International repute.







WHERE IS TELANGANA, ANYWAY?



Telengana, constituent state of south-central India. It is bordered by the states of Maharashtra to the north, Chhattisgarh and Odisha to the northeast, Andhra Pradesh to the southeast and south, and Karnataka to the west. The area of what is now Telangana constituted the north-central and northeastern portions of Andhra Pradesh for almost six decades, but on June 2, 2014, that territory was calved off to form a separate state. The capital of both Telangana and Andhra Pradesh is Hyderabad, in west-central Telangana.














Festivals



In Telangana, one can clearly witness the composite, pluralistic and inclusive culture and traditions. Be it Bathukamma, Sankranthi, Ramzan, Holi, Moharram, or Christmas, the region is a beacon of secular traditions and festive glory. Bathukamma is a colourful and vibrant floral festival of Telangana, celebrated by women, with flowers that grow exclusively in each region. The festival pride of Telangana’s cultural identity.The months from October to February receive a lot of tourists due to pleasant weather. You might see cloudy days but the pleasant atmosphere makes sightseeing in Telangana quite comfortable. Also, the festivals Bathukamma, Vijayadashami or Dussehra, and Navratri are celebrated during this time.
